Stack Overflow

December 16, 2017
Filed under Stray Thoughts

I used to code before internet was something you could have at your home, it wasn’t even the next big thing. You had to go to an internet café to look at yahoo and hotmail, so basically you didn’t. I read books about coding (remember Let us C?) and coded in Turbo C. If you needed to know what the functions are in a library, or why a certain error was popping up, you read the source code, what else could you do?

With the ill-thought move of entering college came the concept of broadband and high speed internet. With the power of man pages and online forums I could just read the description of a function to understand what it did without having to read the source code. This was life changing.

Today with the open source revolution I can literally google up entire code snippets to copy-paste, this is how I built this website. I have no idea how to program in Go but I know how to google and I’m good at troubleshooting, and that’s good enough. With the advent of Gist and Stack Overflow, codes abound, we live in an amateur’s utopia. If you want to do a merge-sort in javascript, hey try googling “merge-sort in javascript”, somebody must surely have implemented it for you, and it’s all free!

The greatest irony is perhaps that Stack Overflow has gone from being a programmer’s worst nightmare to their best friend. Long live open source.

We still need books for Maths and I should probably get back to it. But I wonder for how long…
