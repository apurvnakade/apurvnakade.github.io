Boredom
Filed under Me, February 25, 2018.

I miss being bored.

I miss the feeling of time passing too slowly; it only ever rushes past me hastily these days.

Ever since college, it seems like I’ve constantly been near a screen of some kind. In order to avoid boredom, I’ve watched innumerable series, movies, and videos, read hundreds of books but it all feels like I saw it happening from a distance. The trouble with screens is they only satisfy half of your senses, all screens smell and feel the same, so the memories you form when you are in front of one are fragmentary, incomplete, they aren’t immersive.

Of course, the internet is a technological marvel, but there’s no reason to be plugged-in to it constantly. Recently, I’ve stopped using youtube and netflix almost completely. I’ve installed this extension on chrome for blocking websites. I also try and reply to emails only once a day, and when I do I deal with all of them, ALL OF THEM. This is what an empty gmail inbox looks like in case you were wondering,

This is a very hard thing to do and some days I’m just too tired and succumb to binge-watching but I’m getting better at it. I no longer get anxious over spending a day at home, I watched Netflix for exactly 2 days this month, I check my news and facebook once or twice a day and that too fleetingly, I don’t obsess over unanswered emails during the day at all. I still watch a lot of youtube, but I’m rapidly losing interest in that as well.

It almost feels like a fog getting cleared and the everything is cleaner, brighter, crisper, more vivid and colorful. Nowadays I have more time on my hands than I’m used to but I don’t know if I want to do more things.

Maybe I do, or maybe I’ll just try and get bored again.
