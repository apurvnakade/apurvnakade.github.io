Math can be fun, right?

I love to design and teach interesting maths courses. Sometimes I even get paid for it. Materials for some of my fun courses ...

  • ... at JHU
    • Symmetries & Polynomials (IBL) +
      A two week long class during intersession 2017 where we explored the connection between the unsolvability of the quintic using radicals and symmetric groups.
    • Honors Single Variable Calculus (IBL) +
      A semester long class during Fall 2017 covering material usually covered in standard Calc 1 and Calc 2 classes. Course page contains lots of problem sets.
    • Hitchhiker's Guide to Algebraic Topology +
      A two week long class during intersession 2016 introducing students to various fun aspects of algebraic topology. The course page contains lots of references and student presentations.
  • ... at the amazing place that was the 2018
    • How Curved Is a Potato? +
      A week long class introducing the concepts of Gaussian and Mean curvature using basic multivariable calculus.
    • Cohomology via Sheaves +
      A week long class introducing the concepts of Cech cohomology. Notes contain lots of problems.
    • The Quantum Spring+
      A week long class introducing the connection between Quantum Mechanics and Lie Algebras.
    • Would I ever Lie Group to you? +
      A two day class introducing Lie groups using low dimensional examples. Notes contain lots of problems.
  • ... at the amazing place that was the 2017
    • All things manifoldy +
      A week long class introducing the notion of a manifold, ending in Heegard splittings.
    • Euler Characteristic +
      A week long class presenting the various instances in which Euler Characteristic shows up.
    • Symmetries of Spaces +
      A week long class introducing the concept of Lie groups using low dimensional examples. Notes contain lots of problems.
    • What is Homology? +
      A two day class introducing notion of homology.
    • Representation theory +
      A two day class introducing notions of Representation Theory.
    • Lagrangian and Hamiltonian Mechanics +
      Notes (not by me) for a reading project.
